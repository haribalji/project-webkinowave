Why Wave Energy?

Oceans have the capacity to power the entire world renewably. Among the various renewable options, wave energy remains largely untapped despite its estimated theoretical potential of 41,300 MW in India alone. The major challenge is creating relative motion between the energy extractor and waves in the open ocean.

Existing Approaches and Gaps

  • Point Absorber: Struggles with efficient energy conversion due to varying wave heights and directions.
  • Attenuator: High costs and complex mooring requirements limit widespread adoption.
  • Oscillating Water Column (OWC): Suffers from low efficiency and structural challenges in harsh marine environments.
  • Overtopping Device: Requires large structures that are costly to build and maintain.
  • Oscillating Wave Surge Converter: Mechanical complexity and durability issues in rough sea conditions. These challenges fuel our determination to transform wave energy into a tangible reality.

Our Solution

Kinowave addresses these challenges by eliminating the need for permanent structures or mooring, reducing costs, and increasing efficiency. By leveraging Pascal's principle and the self-leveling effect of water, our interconnected fluid-filled cylinders harness the kinetic energy of ocean waves. The dynamic linear motion between the fluid inside the tanks and the tank surface is efficiently converted into electricity using Wells turbines.

Our Progress

Iteration 1

  • Concept Development:Inspired by Pascal's law and ancient water leveling devices.
  • Initial Prototype:Used plastic bottles connected with tubes to observe the concept. This initial test showed promise but revealed slow water flow rates between the tanks.
  • Key Insight: Realized that the diameters of the tank and connecting tube needed to be the same for efficient water movement.

Iteration 3

  • Real Ocean Testing: Successfully tested the prototype in a real ocean environment. Dry testing showed an efficiency of over 65%, surpassing many existing wave energy generators.
  • Governing Equations and Validation:Worked on and validated the governing equations with IIT Madras.
  • Power Take-Off Mechanism: Researched and integrated highly efficient Wells turbines.
  • Current Status:Tested the Wells turbine in a mini tank and are extracting data to validate its efficiency with support from IIT Madras's wave tank.

Future Plan

We aim to further validate our prototype with the Wells turbine in the wave flume tank, supported by the Grantham Institute. Our plan includes fabricating a 100-watt prototype and, after validation and testing with experts, installing it in real ocean conditions to test its reliability. Our goal is to scale it up to 10 kW, which can power five homes, within the next five years.
